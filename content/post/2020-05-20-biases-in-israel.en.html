The Israeli site offers eight IAT topics for the participants to complete:

  • Race: ‘Black - White’ IAT (N = 4454)
  • Ethnicity: ‘Ashkenazi - Mizrahi’ IAT (N = 9105)
  • Skin-tone: ‘Light Skin - Dark Skin’ IAT (N = 3639)
  • Age: ‘Young - Old’ IAT (N = 3203)
  • Sexuality: ‘Gay - Straight’ IAT (N = 6506)
  • Weight: ‘Fat - Thin’ IAT (N = 5209)
  • Nationalism: ‘USA - Own Country’ IAT (N = 2167)
  • Gender - Science: ‘Male+Science - Female+Liberal arts’ IAT (N = 5709)

Each tasks also included 1-2 self-reported evaluation measures:

  • Self-Reported Direct Preference: Preference between the dominant and non-dominant category (scale: 1-7)
  • Feeling Thermometer: Cold to warm feelings toward category (scale: 0-10)

The tasks also included secondary measures such as perception measures and demographic items. You can check out the data on OSF!

We analyzed the results of each task, from 12 years of data collection (2009-2020) and a total of 39992 participants. We found that implicit bias is robust and persistent: The IAT scores from all tasks revealed that Israelis strongly prefer the dominant social groups (e.g., White people, Ashkenazi people, Young people) and not the non-dominant social groups (Black people, Mizrahi people, Old people).

IAT Scores per Task

All tasks point to a dominant > non-dominant bias. The strongest bias among Israeli participants is regarding Age (Young > Old people) and the smallest bias is regarding Sexuality (Straight > Gay people):

The Ethnicity IAT

This IAT uses categories unique to Israel. Let’s inspect it further. Do people from different ethnicities have different levels of implicit ethnicity-related bias?

We can see that, indeed, the implicit bias varies across the different ethnicities. Still, all ethnicity groups in the Israeli society show the same direction of bias.

Summary

  • Across all tasks, Israelis were biased, explicitly and implicitly, in favor of the dominant social groups.
  • Across all tasks, the bias (preference) was much larger for the implicit than the explicit judgment.
  • The bias stayed the same across the 12 years of data collection.
  • IAT scores: The strongest bias among Israeli participants was regarding Age (Young > Old people) and the smallest bias was regarding Sexuality (Straight > Gay people).
