Summary

  • Across all IAT tasks, both deliberate and automatic evaluation measures point to a preference of the dominant category over the non-dominant category.
  • Across all tasks, the bias (preference) was much larger for the automatic than the deliberate evaluation.
  • Across most of the tasks, the bias stays the same across the 12 years of data collection.
  • IAT scores: The strongest bias among Israeli participants was regarding Age (Young > Old people) and the smallest bias was regarding Sexuality (Straight > Gay people).

Tasks Overview

The IATs

  • Race (‘Black - White’ IAT). This IAT requires the ability to distinguish faces of European and African origin.

  • Ethnicity (‘Ashkenazi - Mizrahi’ IAT). This IAT requires the ability to distinguish names of Ashkenazi (Europian) and Mizrahi (African) origin of Jewish Israelis.

  • Skin-tone (‘Light Skin - Dark Skin’ IAT). This IAT requires the ability to recognize light and dark-skinned faces. It often reveals an automatic preference for light-skin relative to dark-skin.

  • Age (‘Young - Old’ IAT). This IAT requires the ability to distinguish old from young faces. This test often indicates that Americans have automatic preference for young over old.

  • Sexuality (‘Gay - Straight’ IAT). This IAT requires the ability to distinguish words and symbols representing gay and straight people. It often reveals an automatic preference for straight relative to gay people.

  • Weight (‘Fat - Thin’ IAT). This IAT requires the ability to distinguish faces of people who are obese and people who are thin. It often reveals an automatic preference for thin people relative to fat people.

  • Nationalism (USA - Own Country IAT). This IAT often reveals a bias favoring the own country relative to USA.

  • Gender - Science. This IAT often reveals a relative link between liberal arts and females and between science and males.

The Self-Reports

  • Preference (att). Preference between the dominant and non-dominant category - Which statement best describes you? Values: 1 “I strongly prefer NON-DOMINANT CATEGORY to DOMINANT CATEGORY; 7”I strongly prefer DOMINANT CATEGORY to NON-DOMINANT CATEGORY." 4 indicates No preference.

  • Thermometer. Cold to warm feelings toward category. Values: 0 “very cold” 1; 10 “very warm”. Score: difference score of DOMINANT CATEGORY - NON-DOMINANT CATEGORY.

Internal/External motivation to avoid prejudice

  • ems1: Because of today’s standards I try to appear nonprejudiced towards old people.
  • ems4: I attempt to appear nonprejudiced towards old people in order to avoid disapproval from others.

Self-reported feeling thermometer (difference score)

There is a small bias in favor of Young people (in contrast to the large bias in the previous measures). The difference between the current and previous self-report measure might result from the nature of each measure: while the Self-reported preference measure directly compares Young and Old people (as does the IAT), the thermometer does not compare the two categories directly. A comparative context might facilitate bias.
